The 2024 Paris Olympics showcased India's growing competitiveness on the global sports stage. While the country celebrated six medals, several Indian athletes came heartbreakingly close to the podium, finishing in fourth place. These athletes demonstrated remarkable skill and determination, making the nation proud despite narrowly missing out on medals.

Dhiraj Bommadevara and Ankita Bhakat (Archery)

India’s archery duo of Dhiraj Bommadevara and Ankita Bhakat delivered a commendable performance in the mixed team event. They reached the semifinals, showcasing exceptional precision and teamwork. However, in the bronze medal match, they fell just short, finishing in fourth place. Their efforts nonetheless marked India's best-ever finish in Olympic archery, setting a new benchmark for the future.

Men’s 4x400m Relay Team (Athletics)

The Indian men's 4x400m relay team stunned everyone by advancing to the finals and finishing fourth, just missing out on a historic medal. Their performance was especially notable as they set a new national record in the process. The team’s journey from being underdogs to almost clinching a medal captured the attention of athletics fans worldwide.

Vinesh Phogat (Wrestling)

Vinesh Phogat, one of India’s leading wrestlers, competed in the women’s freestyle 53kg category. After a strong start, she advanced to the semifinals but was unable to secure a victory in the bronze medal match, finishing fourth. This was a tough outcome for Phogat, who had been a strong medal contender and is known for her resilience in the sport

Bajrang Punia (Wrestling)

Another close call came in wrestling, where Bajrang Punia, competing in the men’s freestyle 65kg category, finished fourth. Punia, a bronze medalist from the Tokyo 2020 Olympics, was aiming for a second Olympic medal but fell short in the bronze medal match. His efforts were still celebrated as he fought against some of the world’s best wrestlers.


While finishing fourth is often seen as the most difficult position, just missing out on a medal, these Indian athletes exemplified the spirit of perseverance and excellence. Their near-miss performances are a testament to India's growing capabilities in various sports, providing hope and inspiration for future competitions.
