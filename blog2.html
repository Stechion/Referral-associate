India delivered a remarkable performance at the 2024 Paris Olympics, securing a total of six medals—one silver and five bronze. This marks India’s third-best showing in the history of the Summer Games, following Tokyo 2020 and London 2012. Here’s a closer look at the achievements that contributed to this tally:

Neeraj Chopra (Silver, Men’s Javelin Throw)*: Neeraj Chopra, the reigning Olympic champion from Tokyo 2020, added a silver medal to his collection in the men’s javelin throw event. With this, he became the first Indian athlete to win both a gold and a silver medal at the Olympics.

Manu Bhaker (Bronze, Women’s 10m Air Pistol)*: Manu Bhaker made history by becoming the first Indian woman to win an Olympic medal in shooting. She claimed a bronze in the 10m air pistol event, showcasing her resilience after a challenging outing in Tokyo.

Manu Bhaker and Sarabjot Singh (Bronze, Mixed 10m Air Pistol Team)*: In the mixed team event, Manu Bhaker teamed up with Sarabjot Singh to win another bronze, marking India's first team medal in shooting at the Olympics.

Swapnil Kusale (Bronze, Men’s 50m Rifle Three Positions): Swapnil Kusale clinched bronze in the men’s 50m rifle three positions, adding to India's growing legacy in Olympic shooting.

Indian Men’s Hockey Team (Bronze, Men’s Hockey): The men’s hockey team secured a bronze medal by defeating Spain, making it their second consecutive bronze after their podium finish in Tokyo 2020.

Aman Sehrawat (Bronze, Men’s Freestyle 57kg Wrestling)*: Aman Sehrawat, competing in the men’s freestyle 57kg wrestling category, earned a bronze medal. At just 21 years old, he became the youngest Indian to win an Olympic medal.

These achievements highlight India's growing prowess across diverse sports, from traditional strongholds like wrestling and hockey to emerging areas such as shooting.
